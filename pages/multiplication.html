We can solve this problem by breaking down the bottom number into individual columns. We then start in the rightmost column of the bottom number, and multiply each column by the whole top number. So, to start, let's multiply together 25 and 3.

That multiplies to 75, so let's write that underneath the problem in the first available row. Now we move one column to the left, which means that we need to multiply together 25 and 2.

That multiplies to 50. However, we need to remember that we're not just multiplying 25 by 2 here. Since the two was in the "tens" column, that means that we were actually multiplying 25 by 20. So now, we need to multiply our result by 10.

Now we have 500! Since we've now multiplied all columns together, all that's left to do is to add together the numbers under the line to find our total! So let's add together 500 and 75.

And now we have our final answer: 575!
